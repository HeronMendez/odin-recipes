Chocoflan

Description

Chocoflan, also known as "pastel imposible" or "impossible cake," features a layer of creamy caramel flan on the bottom and a moist chocolate cake on top. When baked, the two layers magically switch places, creating a beautiful dessert with contrasting flavors and textures.

Ingredients

For the Caramel

  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup of water

For the flan layer

  • 1 can (14 ounces) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 can (12 ounces) evaporated milk
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the chocolate cake layer

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup buttermilk

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). You'll need a Bundt pan for this recipe.
  2. Start by making the caramel layer: In a small saucepan, combine the sugar and water. Heat over medium heat, swirling the pan occasionally, until the sugar melts and turns a deep amber color. Be careful not to burn it. Immediately pour the caramel into the bottom of your Bundt pan and swirl it around to coat the bottom evenly.
  3. Next, prepare the flan layer: In a blender, combine the sweetened condensed milk, evaporated milk, eggs, and vanilla extract. Blend until smooth. Gently pour this mixture over the caramel layer in the Bundt pan.
  4. For the cake layer, in a large mixing bowl, sift together the flour, cocoa powder, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Add the vegetable oil, eggs, vanilla extract, and buttermilk. Mix until the batter is smooth.
  5. Carefully pour the chocolate cake batter over the flan layer in the Bundt pan, ensuring you do so gently to prevent the layers from mixing too much.
  6. Cover the Bundt pan with aluminum foil and place it inside a larger, ovenproof dish. Add hot water to the larger dish until it reaches halfway up the side of the Bundt pan, creating a water bath.
  7. Bake in the preheated oven for 1 hour and 30 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the chocolate cake layer comes out clean.
  8. Remove the chocoflan from the oven and let it cool in the water bath for about 30 minutes.
  9. Once cooled, carefully invert the chocoflan onto a serving platter. The caramel and flan layers will be on top, and the chocolate cake layer on the bottom.
  10. Chill the chocoflan in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours or until serving.

To serve, slice the chocoflan and enjoy the creamy, caramel-infused flan paired with the moist chocolate cake. It's a delightful dessert that's sure to impress your guests!
